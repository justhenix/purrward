# Purrward Design Guardrails

This skill enforces the Purrward design system rules and prevents drifting into generic AI/SaaS/dev-dashboard styles.

## Aesthetic Boundary & Hard Bans

Do not use these in Purrward UI:

- **No monospace fonts anywhere** in user-facing UI.
- **No dot-live status badges** (e.g. `AI · LIVE`, `.live`, `system.online`).
- **No AI-generated image assets** or fake photorealism. Use wireframe placeholders for missing assets.
- **No generic AI/SaaS landing page structure**: badge, giant gradient H1, vague subtext, two CTAs, three cards.
- **No purple/blue AI gradients** as the main identity.
- **No glassmorphism** as default card styling.
- **No neon glows, cyber shadows, aura blobs**, or holographic borders.
- **No left-border status cards** unless they communicate a real semantic state.
- **No fake metrics** (e.g. `10M+ pets`, `99.9% accuracy`) without product proof.
- **No emoji-only navigation**.
- **No generic Lucide icon tiles** as the main personality.
- **No developer-console panels** in normal user mode.
- **No medical app coldness**; must feel supportive and calm.
- **No gacha game chaos** that overwhelms care routines.

## Typography & Copy Guardrail

- Display Font: **Quicksand** (round display).
- Sans Font: **Inter** (body & controls).
- Monospace: **Banned**.
- Copy Tone: Use concrete pet-care language (`Log today's feeding`, `Upload a bowl photo`, `Ask about vomiting`). Avoid AI spectacle (`Supercharge your pet wellness`, `Unlock seamless AI-powered care`).

## Motion & Interaction

- Gentle and useful micro-animations (button press, card lift, verification progress, cat mood change, points increment).
- Avoid: random hover scaling on every card, scroll animations everywhere, constant bouncing, confetti spam, neon glow.

## Page Review Checklist

Before finalizing any screen:
1. Does this feel like cozy cat care, not generic AI SaaS?
2. Does every visual choice support a real pet-care moment?
3. Is the cat/avatar/emotional layer present where useful?
4. Are rewards secondary to healthy routines, not gambling-first?
5. Is AI Vet presented as triage/help, not magical diagnosis?
6. Are emergency states calm, clear, and serious?
7. Is the design mobile-first?
8. Are there any accidental tech-dashboard patterns?
9. Are there any monospace fonts?
10. Does this still follow design tokens?
